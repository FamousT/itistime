PROFESSOR YEMI OSINBAJO, SAN, GCON





THE MIND OF A GENIUS; THE HEART OF A STATESMAN

Born into the family of Opeoluwa Osinbajo on the 8th of March 1957, Professor Yemi Osinbajo GCON had his primary education at Corona primary School, in Lagos, before going on to attend Igbobi College Yaba Lagos, from 1969 to 1975.

Even as a young scholar, Yemi Osinbajo was singled out for his exemplary oratory skills and academic aptitude with numerous awards like the State Merit Award (1971); the School Prize for English Oratory (1972); Adeoba Prize for English Oratory (1972-1975); Elias Prize for Best Performance in History (WASC, 1973); School Prize for Literature (HSC, 1975); and African Statesman Intercollegiate Best Speaker's Prize (1974).

Thereafter, Prof. Osinbajo studied for his undergraduate degree at the University of Lagos between 1975-1978 where he obtained a Second-Class Honours (Upper Division) Degree in Law. Here, he also won the Graham-Douglas Prize for Commercial Law. In 1979, after completing the mandatory one-year professional training at the Nigerian Law School, he was admitted to practice as a Barrister and Solicitor of Nigeria's Supreme Court.

In 1980, Prof. Osinbajo went on to attend the London School of Economics, where he obtained a Master of Laws degree. In 1981, at the age of 23 he was employed as a Law lecturer at the University of Lagos, Nigeria.

In 1981, at the age of 23 he was employed as a Law lecturer at the University of Lagos, Nigeria. From 1983 to 1986, he was a Senior Lecturer of Law at the University of Lagos. From 1988 to 1992, he was an Adviser (Legal Advice and Litigation) to the Attorney-General and Minister of Justice, Bola Ajibola. During his tenure as the Attorney General and Commissioner of Justice from 1999-2007, Osinbajo implemented significant judicial reforms in Lagos State, addressing important areas such as Judges' recruitment, remuneration, training and discipline. He also established the Directorate for Citizen' Rights (DCR), which provides quick and free access to justice for all residents of Lagos State. In addition, he addressed access to justice for the poor by establishing appropriate institutions in the Office of the Public Defender (OPD).

From 1997 to 1999 he was made Professor of Law and Head of Department of Public Law, University of Lagos. From 1999 to 2007, Osinbajo was a Member of Cabinet, Lagos State Ministry of Justice, also Attorney-General and Commissioner for Justice. In 2007, Osinbajo was made Senior Partner at Simmons Cooper Partners (Barristers and Solicitors), Nigeria, and from 2007 to 2013 Osinbajo was once again employed as a Professor of Law, Department of Public Law, Faculty of Law, University of Lagos.

Over the following years, driven by a deep-seated passion to touch the lives of people, Osinbajo kickstarted several charity initiatives like The Destiny Trust, Bethesda School of the Blind, The Justice Research Institute and Orderly Society Trust and Liberty School Ikota, earning him the unwavering support of Nigerians everywhere. These charity initiatives actively cater to those at the bottom of the pyramid with free health care facilities, free education, free legal services, social rehabilitation, skill acquisition, and free shelter for people without accommodation.

POLITICS

After the formation of the All Progressives Congress (APC) in 2013, Osinbajo was tasked, alongside some other notable Nigerians, to design and produce a manifesto for the new political party. This assignment resulted in the presentation of the "Roadmap to a New Nigeria", a document published by APC as its manifesto if elected to power.

On 17 December 2014 the presidential candidate of the All-Progressives Congress, retired General Muhammadu Buhari announced Osinbajo as his running mate and Vice-Presidential candidate for the 2015 General elections.

During the electoral campaigns, Yemi Osinbajo who was the running mate of the APC presidential candidate, held numerous town hall meetings across the country as against the popular rallies that many Nigerians and their politicians were used to; breaking down the APC manifesto of office into promises that include, giving a school child a meal per day, and subsequently creating jobs for those who would implement it.






TIME IN OFFICE




Yemi Osinbajo assumed office after taking the oath of office on 29 May 2015 at the Eagle Square, Abuja. As the Vice President of Nigeria, he has over the course of his tenure overseen the Economic Management Team, reporting as well as making recommendations to the President who then takes the final decision based on the recommendations made.

As a result of Osinbajo's commendable legal antecedents as a Commissioner for Justice in Lagos State for eight years, he has been able to consistently contribute to the reform of the judicial system at the national level.

ACTING PRESIDENT

on 9th May 2017, President Muhammadu Buhari by a written declaration, informed the President of the Senate and Speaker of the House of Representatives of his decision to embark on an extended medical leave, subsequently conferring Professor Yemi Osinbajo with the role of Acting President of Nigeria.

On 29 May 2019, Professor Yemi Osinbajo again took his oath of office at Eagle Square in the capital of Abuja, heralding the beginning of a second term in office as the Vice president of Nigeria.

Vice President Yemi Osinbajo is married to Dolapo (née Soyode) Osinbajo, a granddaughter of the late sage, Chief Obafemi Awolowo. They have three children - two daughters, Damilola, Kanyinsola and a son, Fiyinfoluwa Osinbajo.
