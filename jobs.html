Jobs and Opportunity

With a large youth population, Nigeria is in a strong position to build a workforce that is skilled, competent and successful.
To do this, Yemi Osinbajo will prioritise the Manufacturing, Technology, Agriculture and Construction sectors.


In prioritising these sectors, Yemi Osinbajo will focus on:

  • Supporting young people to acquire relevant skills;
  • Building infrastructure to support the prioritised sectors;
  • Creating a supportive business environment; and
  • Where necessary, provide or facilitate financing for businesses and young people to thrive in entrepreneurship;
  • Promoting integrated rural development

Agriculture


The Agriculture sector provides 35% of employment in Nigeria and contributes an average of 30% to the GDP of Nigeria. Yemi Osinbajo’s strategy to develop the agricultural sector will:

  1. Build Markets
  2. Facilitate Finance
  3. Improve Processing
  4. Increase Yields


His policies will:

  • Increase the funding towards Research and Development to generate and commercialise new agricultural technologies fit for the Nigerian clime to the maximisation of our Agricultural potential.
  • Increase funding to maintain and increase the capacity of old farms and create new farms to ensure food security for the nation as well as ensure agricultural development programmes.
  • Integrate agricultural commodity value chains into the broader supply chain of Nigerian and global industry, driving job growth, increasing the contribution of agriculture to wealth creation, and enhancing the capacity of the country to earn foreign exchange from agricultural export.

Manufacturing


Industrialising our economy will remain a key focus and light industry including the following will be prioritized: petrochemicals, plastics, recycling, garment-making.


Therefore, Yemi Osinbajo will:

  • Establish Special Production and Processing Centres to support agriculture processing and enrich exports across the country.
  • Provide energy sources including harnessing natural gas as a viable transition energy source to power our industrialisation endeavours.
  • Expand the availability of development finance to support manufacturing.
  • Facilitate easy access to raw materials and intermediate goods.

Construction


Accelerate ongoing investment and construction of Social Housing to support the expansion of direct and indirect jobs in the construction of homes and the local manufacture of building materials.

Technology


In the age of technology, disruption is at the core of growth.
With emerging technologies progressing at a rapid pace there is an opportunity to leverage and leapfrog on virtually every component for growth and development.

Yemi Osinbajo will ensure that Nigeria takes the lead and leverage technology as an enabler sector.

Policy, Enabling Environment and Governance Reforms


Accelerate ongoing investment and construction of Social Housing to support the expansion of direct and indirect jobs in the construction of homes and the local manufacture of building materials.

He will:

  • Continue the implementation of the Investment in Creative and Digital Enterprises (i-DICE) Programme;
  • Institute Government policy, regulation and legislation to support the ease of doing business for technology and creative sector businesses;
  • Review of the Public Prcurement Act to enable start-up and growth stage companies bid for Federal Government contracts;
  • Establish a start-up visa category to attract the best and talent from across the world that seeks to invest in Nigeria’s technology and creativity sector. The visa will be valid for initial period of five years with the possibility for a further five-year extension based on the performance of the investments;
  • Policy support for ecosystem investment in the technology sector with specific focus on the advantages of new technologies including cryptocurrencies, blockchain technology, artificial intelligence and machine learning, drone manufacturing and operations;
  • Coordinate an Africa-wide policy direction for the growth of technology and creative sector companies, leveraging the provisions of the African Continental Free Trade Area Agreement (AFCFTA);
  • Provide workspace vouchers for 20,000 start-ups annually, working with Enterprise Support Organisations, Innovation Centres, and Technology Hubs across the 36 states of Nigeria and the FCT;
  • Define framework and criteria for the designation of Technology and Creativity Free Trade Zones. Work with the private sector to deliver the first zone in each of the 6 geopolitical zones in Nigeria.

To rapidly develop Nigeria and truly make her great again, start by choosing a better leader.
