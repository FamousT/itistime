ABOUT PROF. YEMI OSINBAJO, SAN, GCON

On the 8th of March 1957, Yemi Osinbajo was born to Opeoluwa and Olubisi Osinbajo at Creek hospital in Lagos, Nigeria. He had his primary school education at Corona Primary School, Lagos, and then proceeded to Igbobi College, Lagos, for his secondary school education from 1969 to 1975.

From 1975 to 1978, Yemi Osinbajo was an undergraduate student at the Faculty of Law, University of Lagos. He obtained a Second Class Honours (Upper Division) Degree in Law, and was awarded the Graham-Douglas Prize for Commercial Law at the end of his studies. At the age of 23, Yemi was engaged as a Lecturer of the Faculty of Law, University of Lagos, and was made a Senior Lecturer two years later. In 1997, he was made a Professor of Law and the Head of the Department of Public Law, at the University of Lagos.

In 1999, Yemi was appointed the secretary of a Transition Work Group that was tasked with producing strategies on transitioning from military to democratic rule in Lagos State, shortly after the 1999 General Elections.
