Investing in People

Education


Nigeria will be one big Learning Centre. We will build a national ethos- introduce an educational paradigm that is built on an orientation around the values of hard work, honesty, patriotism and service.


Yemi Osinbajo will:

  • Halve the number of children out of school in four years through existing programmes that target out of school children;
  • Intentionally invest in the development of practical, creative and innovative skills for young Nigerians that will be the catalyst for Nigeriaâ€™s emerging knowledge economy, through;
  • Review and implement policies that will provide a strong foundation for young Nigerians in Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts and Mathematics (STEAM) and other related skills positioning them for digital and economic present and future.
  • Expand and accelerate teacher training
  • Improve school infrastructure and expand the number of classrooms across the country
  • Continue to improve the training and welfare of teachers, lecturers and instructors as a priority.

Skills and Human Capital Development


Yemi Osinbajo will make Nigeria a destination for technology and creative sector skills.
He will:

  • Provide sustainable financing options to digital and creative sector skills and talent providers across the country.
  • Establish one Digital and Creativity Sector Skills Centre of Excellence per State. Each centre of excellence will be a collaboration between private sector implementation experts and an institution of higher learning, with a capacity to train a minimum of 25,000 individuals in intermediate level digital and creative sector skills annually.
  • Work with the private sector on the development of capacity for skills development in blockchain technologies, artificial intelligence, machine learning, data analytics, data science, animation, script writing, cinematography etc in higher institutions across the country and via the centres of excellence.

Social Investments


Over the last 7 years, Nigeria has instituted and implemented the largest social safety net programme in Africa. The programme has supported the poorest families, job seekers, traders and artisans in the country through the following:

  • Conditional Cash Transfers to the poor families
  • N-Power jobs for young jobseekers
  • School Feeding for primary school children
  • GEEP soft loans for traders, artisans and farmers


It is now time to fulfil the promise of lifting 100 million Nigerians out of poverty within this decade and commit wholeheartedly to making sure that no Nigerian is left behind

Health


To strengthen resilience against unforeseen events like global pandemics, Yemi Osinbajo will:

  • Expand Universal Health Insurance to meet the needs of the poorest citizens
  • Hasten the implementation of the Basic Healthcare Provision Fund to achieve at least a 65% increase in the share of the population covered by primary healthcare

To rapidly develop Nigeria and truly make her great again, start by choosing a better leader.
