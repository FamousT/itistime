Infrastructure

Over the last 7 years, there has been an unprecedented investment in Nigeria’s infrastructure. Yemi Osinbajo will continue these enabling investments until the whole of Nigeria is completely integrated, such that people, goods and services can flow freely within, as well as to and from Nigeria.


To be competitive, the economy must be a connected one. Yemi Osinbajo will consolidate upon the progress and expand Nigeria’s infrastructure to support, strengthen both hard and virtual connectivity through the following means:


  • Rail: Expand upon the Lagos-Ibadan Standard Gauge and the Abuja-Kaduna rail line by actualizing a strategic rail development that connects all major economic centres across Nigeria. Key priority areas will be completion of the Lagos- Kano and Lagos- Calabar rail projects,

  • Roads & Bridges: Continue to utilise innovative mechanisms like the Sukuk Bonds to expand our roads and bridges network.

  • Broadband Infrastructure: Increase broadband penetration by 20% annually over the next 4 years;
    • Mandate the implementation of a fibre duct for every new road construction or reconstruction on every federal road in the country. This is to aid the expansion of Nigeria’s fibre optic broadband coverage across the country;
    • Implement Nigeria’s broadband policy and 5G policy in collaboration with the private sector for increased access;
    • Support the expansion of broadband coverage and last mile broadband infrastructure to every institution of higher learning, healthcare institutions, and other public centres of strategic interest, through public-private sector partnerships to ensure sustainability.

  • Airports: We will improve the maintenance of and boost the operational efficiency of our four major international airports through the swift concessioning of - Abuja, Lagos, Port Harcourt and Kano airports in the next four years.

  • Seaports: Improve efficiency at Apapa ports, especially for export activity. Open up existing ports in the South East and South South regions of the country to ease exports.

To rapidly develop Nigeria and truly make her great again, start by choosing a better leader.
